## Raw Sources

## Theme

Korean numbers: Sino-Korean and native Korean forms, with their common usage contexts.

## Goals

- Distinguish the two main Korean number systems.
- Memorize the visible 1-10 forms in both systems.
- Learn common situations where each number system is used.
- Capture the additional higher-value forms shown on the slide.

## What The Slide Teaches

- Left side, Sino-Korean: `일` through `십`, plus `백`, `천`, `만`
- Right side, native Korean: `하나` through `열`, plus `스물`, `서른`, `마흔`, `쉰`, `예순`, `일흔`, `여든`, `아흔`

## Usage Notes

- Sino-Korean:
  - 수의 차례, 번호, 단위
  - telephone numbers, bus route numbers, height, weight, address number, years, months, minutes, seconds, and prices
- Native Korean:
  - 개수, 횟수, 크기 등
  - when counting things or people

## Focus For Review

- Do not mix the two decks mentally; review them separately.
- For Sino-Korean, pay special attention to the larger units `백`, `천`, `만`.
- For native Korean, focus on the tens forms from `스물` to `아흔`, since those are less regular than 1-10.
